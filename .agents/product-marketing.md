## Product Overview
**Brand architecture:** Hububb is "Everything around Stays," the parent brand and root identity (the Revolut "Banking and Beyond" model). Three standalone products sit under it, each marketed on its own:
- **Hububb Host** (hububb.com/hosts): the Host OS. Host tooling. The only product sold today, and the focus of this file.
- **Hububb Stay**: a mid-term channel for 28 to 180 day stays, with its own site and products (Nomad Pass, corporate bookings). In development, not built yet. Out of scope. Never market or imply these features exist.
- **Hububb Connect**: the work app for service providers. Live in the App Store. Sibling product, out of scope here.

This file is the marketing context for Hububb Host only. The parent identity and the siblings are recorded for laddering and guardrails: the engine markets Host, and must never claim Stay's or Connect's capabilities.

**One-liner:** Everything a host needs, in one app.

**Load-bearing lines** (verbatim, never paraphrased, from BRAND_TOV):
- Positioning: The end-to-end Host OS for flexible stays. From cleaning to autopilot. Bookings come in. Hububb delivers.
- Headline: Stays are easy to book. Hard to deliver. Built for the hard part.

**What it does:** Hububb Host consolidates the 5-8 disconnected tools a rental needs (channel sync, a unified inbox, cleaning and turnover coordination, a vetted service marketplace, dynamic pricing, access management, and clear financials) into one operating system for hosts. At parent level Hububb is the sync layer across hosts, guests, and service providers; Hububb Host is the host-facing product within that.

**Product category:** Host OS for flexible stays. The shelf hosts search on is short-term rental management, PMS, channel manager, "Airbnb management tool." Hububb Host sits one level above that shelf, not on it.

**Product type:** Vertical operating system for short-term rental hosts, inside a three-sided ecosystem (hosts, service providers, guests). Hosts are the primary user and payer.

**Business model:** Modular and usage-based: pay only for what you run. A base subscription, then add pieces a la carte (GBP): channel connectors from £5/month each (£20 for all; Airbnb free, Booking free on your own account), dynamic pricing £15/month, access arrangements £5/month per smart device, plus AI features as add-ons (AI guest messaging is live; more AI features rolling out). A per-unit bundle covering all channels and AI features is planned.

## Target Audience
**Who they are:** Short-term rental hosts, from the 1-property side-hustler to the roughly 10-property pro-am. Mostly owner-operators, often run as a couple or a small partnership. The same product serves both ends of that range: it assumes competence and explains only what needs explaining.
**Where:** London at launch. It is the only market where the full service stack (cleaning, providers, turnovers) can be delivered today, so it is the serviceable audience for now.
**Decision-maker:** The host. An owner-operator buying decision, no committee, no procurement.
**Primary use case:** Run rentals like a pro: earn more, with less day-to-day effort.

**Jobs to be done:**
The hire: hosts hire Hububb to run their short-term rental reliably and professionally, without constant stress or micromanagement, so they can earn stable income while keeping control and peace of mind.

Core jobs:
- Run the operation day to day without things falling through the cracks: cleaning, turnovers, maintenance, last-minute issues.
- Deliver a consistent, professional guest experience without being always-on.
- Know and protect profitability: see costs against revenue, stop the silent leaks.
- Carry less in their head: trade constant low-level stress ("did I forget something?") for reliable oversight and reassurance. (The cognitive job. Often ignored, often the deciding one.)

Anti-jobs (actively avoided):
- Becoming a full-time manager.
- Depending on unreliable people.
- Juggling 10 different tools.
- Constant guest interruptions.
- Feeling dumb or unprofessional.

**Use cases:**
- An investment-apartment owner whose "passive income" quietly became a full-time job. (The strongest.)
- A host scaling from 1 to 3 properties who needs operations that do not break under growth.

*Parked (maybe later): a host who priced out a property management company and kept self-managing.*

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Running a short-term rental quietly turns into a stressful second job. The host becomes the operating system: dispatcher, inbox, bookkeeper, and quality control all at once, held together with WhatsApp, one trusted cleaner, the platform inboxes, the bank balance, and constant vigilance. It works until it does not, and it never stops asking for attention.

**Why alternatives fall short:** What hosts use today is mostly improvisation, not tooling: messaging apps, one trusted cleaner, screenshots, a spreadsheet, gut feeling. It is fast and familiar, which is why they reach for it. But it has no structure and no accountability, every reliable part is a single point of failure (the cleaner who cancels, the knowledge that lives in one person's head), and none of it scales past a property or two. The software-shaped answers do not close the gap either: legacy PMS tools are built for the booking and admin side, not the service side. (Full competitor map, including where property managers fit, in the next section.)

**What it costs them:**
- Time: evenings and weekends, and the feeling of being permanently on call.
- Money: costs that creep silently, revenue left on the table, no clear view of where the money leaks.
- Growth: the business cannot scale, because the operation only runs as long as the host runs it.
- Mental load: the rental lives in their head all day, a low background hum of "did I forget something?"
- Risk: missed cleanings, no-shows, missing keys, bad reviews, the surprises that cost money or reputation.

**Emotional tension:** The "passive income" that became a full-time job they never applied for. The fear of losing control. The sense of being chained to the business they built to set them free. Underneath all of it, what they want is not more features. It is to feel in control without being involved in everything.

## Competitive Landscape
*Reframed around the job, not the tool. Hosts do not shop for "STR software" and pick Hububb over a rival app. They cope, with a pile of habits, people, and apps, and the real competitor for each job is whatever they reach for today.*

| Job | What hosts hire today | Why it fails | Why Hububb wins |
|-----|----------------------|--------------|-----------------|
| Daily ops | WhatsApp, calls, notes in their head | No structure, no accountability, things get forgotten, cannot scale | Structure without friction |
| Cleaning and turnover | "My cleaner," verbal agreements | Single point of failure, no backup, no quality check | Reliability and backups |
| Guest comms | Platform inboxes, the host's phone, or a standalone unified-inbox tool | A point tool unifies messages but leaves the rest fragmented: still living in several platform dashboards, still interrupt-driven, no delegation | Messages and listing management in one place, instead of logging into five platforms. A buffer, not just another inbox |
| Risk and surprises | Anxiety, constant checking, being present | High stress, not scalable, still misses things | Visibility, alerts, accountability |
| Finances | The bank balance, OTA payout screens | No unit economics, no cost attribution, no idea where money leaks | Clarity and insight (know you're profitable, not just feel it) |
| Scale | Property managers | Take 15-25% of gross, and the host's control with it | Control and margin |
| Support | Friends, other hosts, ad hoc | Informal, inconsistent, no real backstop | A professional safety net |

**The strategic point:** there is no single rival app to beat. The competitor is the status quo, the host's pile of improvised coping. Among actual software, the nearest analog is Hospitable: a similar host-tooling app, not a full head-to-head competitor. Hububb's edge over it is being modular (pay only for what you use), a radically simpler and more intuitive interface (near-foolproof), and real visibility. The legacy-PMS shelf sits further out: feature-heavy, built for booking and admin, not the service side. The through-line holds: every other option says "replace your tool with ours." Hububb says "we absorb the burden."

## Differentiation
**The USP is opinionated simplicity.** Hububb is the simplest host toolset that does only what's core to running a rental, without bloat or the need to wire up other accounts and connectors. The edge is not feature count, and not being best at one job. It's judgment: deciding exactly what a host needs, building only that, and making it work as one. The Linear and Apple posture applied to hosting, the taste to leave things out.

**Key differentiators:**
- Restraint is the feature. Only the core, shipped well, not a settings maze most hosts never touch. Modular, so you add the channels, services, and AI you actually use, never a bundle of things you don't.
- Self-contained. Everything you need is native: channels, messaging, cleaning, pricing, access, financials. Nothing external to stitch together, no living in five platform dashboards.
- Built for the service side. Connect, a vetted Services marketplace, and turnovers scheduled automatically against reservations. The part the PMS shelf and point tools ignore, and why services plus software beat software alone.
- The calm layer. Near-foolproof simplicity plus real visibility means in control without being involved in everything. The payoff is less, not more. The deciding factor, and why a host will pay a premium.

**How we do it differently:** Every other option says "replace your tool with ours," or "here's every feature you could want." Hububb does the opposite: only what's core, made simple, working as one, with the host choosing how much to hand over, from a single pain point to full autopilot.

**Why that's better:** No bloat to learn, nothing external to wire up, and the core fronts reinforce each other instead of fragmenting. Reliability becomes systemic, profitability visible, the mental load lighter.

**Why customers choose us:** Not the most features, not the best at one thing. The best at being the simplest tool that covers the core and just works, without bloat and without a stack to assemble. Judgment and restraint, sold as peace of mind.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push** (what drives them off the status quo): hosting has quietly become a second job. WhatsApp chaos, the cleaner who cancels with no backup, midnight guest messages, pricing by guesswork, no clear view of profit, and the constant low hum of "did I forget something?" The passive income became a full-time job they never applied for.
**Pull** (what draws them to Hububb): the calm layer. One opinionated, simple system that makes reliability systemic instead of personal, and lets them feel in control without being involved in everything. Less to manage, not another dashboard. And they can start with the one front that hurts most, not a rip-and-replace.
**Habit** (what keeps them stuck): the improvised stack works well enough, it's familiar, and it's basically free. The cleaner is a personal relationship. The operation runs in shifts with a partner. And the knowledge lives in their own head, which feels safer than handing it over.
**Anxiety** (what worries them about switching): Will my listings actually import and sync? Is there a learning curve? Can I trust automation with my guests and my pricing? It's new, so is it proven? The modular "start with one pain point" path exists to shrink exactly these fears.

## Brand Voice
*The spine is BRAND_TOV.md. This is the working summary the skills need; read that doc for the full treatment.*

**Voice attributes** (constant everywhere). The "but not" does the work:
| We are | But not |
|--------|---------|
| Plain | dumbed-down |
| Operational | bureaucratic |
| Empathetic | performative |
| Restrained | safe |
| Specific | generic |

**Personality dial** (settings to defend, not drift from):
- Formal to casual: 3. Plainspoken, neither stiff nor familiar.
- Serious to playful: 2. Mostly serious, occasional dry wit.
- Direct to diplomatic: 2. Direct, we don't soften unless we mean it.
- Plain to poetic: 3. Plain by default, occasional poetic restraint.
- Confident to humble: 3. State what's true without overclaiming.
- Authoritative to conversational: 4. We talk with hosts, not at them. (The deliberate deviation: infrastructure brands usually sit at 1 to 2 here.)

**Two tests, every line:**
- The earn test: does every word earn its place? Cut what doesn't.
- The own-it test: could only Hububb have written this? If anyone in the category could, rewrite.

**Writing principles** (the operational ones):
- Lead with a fact, not a rhetorical question. No "Are you tired of."
- Observation over assertion: "Most hosts juggle five apps to run one rental," not "Hububb makes hosting effortless."
- Hard close: end on the shortest sentence in the paragraph.
- Warmth lives in what you don't say. No emoji, no exclamation, no "we're so excited."
- Specific over abstract: numbers, mechanics, observed truths beat promises.

**Tone modulation:**
- By audience: hosts warmest (peers, partners in the operation), guests very warm (hospitality is the product), service providers coolest (functional respect, the work is the message).
- By moment: onboarding (more explanatory), steady-state (default, minimal), error (direct, softened, "couldn't" not "failed"), loss (brief acknowledgment, immediate next step), celebration (the most restrained, one quiet line, never confetti).

**Hostie** (the AI agent) speaks in first person with this exact voice and modulation. Not a character, no separate personality. "I'll handle this," not "Hi! I'm your friendly assistant!"

**References:** Stripe (precision with the occasional wink), Linear (rhythm, restraint), Vercel, Cursor, Tobi Lutke, Uber (one voice at scale), Kindred (warm without performing).
**Anti-reference:** the generic PMS shelf. A line is off-brand if it could survive a find-and-replace onto any competitor's site.

## Goals
**Business goal:** Validate Hububb Host with the first London hosts. Acquire them hand-to-hand (team-led, manual), fix friction in real time, lock the positioning on real feedback, and instrument analytics. This early cohort is what makes the Q3 paid-acquisition push real rather than a guess.
**First milestone:** 100 active hosts, then re-evaluate. "Active" means a host actually using the product: at least one channel connected, or at least one clean per week. A leading indicator that the product is delivering, not vanity sign-ups.
**Conversion action:** A host signs up, completes onboarding (connects a channel, imports listings), and crosses the active bar above. AI-supported onboarding, not concierge. North Star metric (internal): Monthly Serviced Stays.
**Current metrics:** Pre-launch, none yet. Serviceable market: London only, for now.
